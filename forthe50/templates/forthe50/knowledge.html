Understanding Modern Slavery and Human Trafficking

Modern slavery and human trafficking are grave violations of human rights. Millions are affected globally, including in the UK and Ireland. Learn how to recognize, report, and combat this crisis.

What Are Modern Slavery and Human Trafficking?

Modern slavery involves individuals being exploited and unable to leave due to threats, coercion, or deception. Common forms include:

  • Forced labor
  • Debt bondage
  • Domestic servitude
  • Sexual exploitation
  • Criminal activities

Human trafficking refers to the recruitment, transportation, and exploitation of victims through fraud, force, or coercion.

How to Recognize the Signs

Recognizing the signs of modern slavery is critical. Here are key indicators:

  • Malnourishment and poor living conditions
  • Fearful or withdrawn behavior
  • Restricted access to personal documents
  • Long working hours with little or no pay

Industry-specific signs include:

Industry Signs
Agriculture Overcrowded housing, little or no pay for long hours
Construction Lack of protective gear, unsafe conditions
Hospitality Anxious employees, dependence on tips, no contracts
Nail Bars/Beauty Young workers, submissive to managers, undertrained

How You Can Help

Here are ways you can contribute to the fight against modern slavery:

Volunteer

Many organizations rely on volunteers for:

  • Awareness campaigns and fundraising
  • Support and mentorship for survivors
  • Training sessions to recognize and report slavery

Donate

Financial contributions support rescue missions, victim care, and advocacy programs. Consider donating to:

  • Hope for Justice
  • Salvation Army
  • Unseen UK

How to Report Modern Slavery

If you suspect modern slavery or human trafficking, report it through these trusted channels:

  • Modern Slavery Helpline: 0800 0121 700
  • Police (Emergencies): Call 999
  • Police (Non-Emergencies): Call 101
  • Crimestoppers (Anonymous): 0800 555 111
